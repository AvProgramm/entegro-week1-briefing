Positioning

Who buys - four personas and a possible new niche

The Sufferer

Ongoing gut issues; capsules didn't work. Wants proof: strain science, testimonials, the money-back guarantee. Found via search and practitioners.

The Optimiser

Gen Z / millennial wellness-router. Discovers on TikTok and Instagram; wants clean label and convenience; natural subscriber.

The Family Buyer

Parent buying for the household - Junior as the entry point. Values safety, allergen-free formulation, and the guarantee.

The Referred

Sent by a health practitioner or wellness centre. Highest trust, least price-sensitive; served by the business-partner channel.

Possible new niche: weight-loss medication companions

People on glucagon-like peptide-1 (Ozempic-style) medications managing digestive side effects and new eating routines - a fast-growing group that already thinks daily about gut comfort. To validate in Week 3.

Four personas: Sufferer (needs proof), Optimiser (social discovery, subscribes),

Go-To-Market Today

Where control, reach and margin actually live

ChannelTypeUpsideTrade-off
Official website Direct-to-consumer Most control over pricing, branding, promotions and customer data; starter kits and subscription benefits reward loyalty Entegro carries shipping, advertising, fulfilment and customer service itself
Amazon Third-party marketplace Huge customer base and strong product visibility Less control of the customer relationship; intense price competition
Major retailers First-party (retailer buys wholesale) Broad retail reach and brand credibility The retailer controls pricing, promotions and customer data
People-based recommendations Consultants, affiliates, referrals High trust through personal recommendation; reaches specific groups Hard to guarantee sales quality, consistency and scale

Working conclusion: the official website is the most favourable channel - most control over pricing, customer relationships and repeat purchase - with Amazon and retail adding reach.

Four channel types, four trade-offs - control versus reach.
Website: most control and best repeat-purchase economics, but Entegro does all the work.
Amazon and retail buy reach at the cost of control and margin. People-based builds trust but scales unevenly.
Working conclusion: lead with the official website; use the others for reach.

United States Market Landscape

Under pressure, buyers sort products into three piles

  • Value is judged beyond price - ingredient quality, transparency, ethical impact and product relevance all count, not just the cheapest option.
  • Spending splits into essentials, small treats, and non-essentials - and the non-essential pile gets cut first.
  • Wellness products survive when they feel essential - part of daily wellbeing, not an optional add-on.

Strategic Implications

Where Weeks 2-4 should dig

  • Clarify the core buyer - sufferers, optimisers, parents, or practitioner-referred? Pick, don't average.
  • Make evidence unmissable - third-party testing and strain science on every surface.
  • Reduce trial friction - TO GO packs, first-order discount, the 30-day challenge.
  • Recession-proof the value story - about $1.17 a day, subscription savings, family use cases.
  • Prioritise channels - partners build trust; direct-to-consumer subscription drives margin. Don't grow everything equally.
Five moves: pick one core buyer; make the evidence unmissable;
lower trial friction; lead with the $1.17-a-day value story;
prioritise channels - partners build trust, subscription drives margin.
Our Recommendation

Make Flourish the daily wellness habit - sold direct

  • Position as a daily essential - everyday wellness for the whole household, not an optional supplement to try once.
  • Lead with the official website - the channel with the most control over pricing, customer data and repeat purchase; push starter kits and subscription benefits.
  • Differentiate by taste and occasion - Junior's milder taste for first-timers and families; TO GO for on-the-go; same trusted formula everywhere.
  • Make credibility unmissable - clean label, third-party testing on every batch, and the 30-day money-back guarantee, front and centre.
  • Meet younger buyers on social - short-form gut-health content for Gen Z and millennial wellness culture, always tied to credible, clear benefits.

Weeks 2-4 will pressure-test these against downturn case studies and competitor moves before the final strategy.

Five recommendations: daily-essential positioning; website-first channel strategy;
